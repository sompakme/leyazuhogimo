Sylvester Stallone's daughter Sistine makes modelling debut

    Sylvester Stallone may have conquered the world of Hollywood, but it seems daughter Sistine Rose is set to pave the way to her own stardom as she makes her modelling debut. The 16-year-old has landed her first major editorial spread, looking the epitome of chic in a cream dress with buttons and a belted waist.

    Sistine has landed her first major editorial spread

    Despite her father's international fame for his roles in films such as Rocky and Rambo, Sistine revealed that she doesn't think the acting genes have passed down the family. "I inherited my dad's sense of humour and work ethic," she told the publication. "I'm a horrible actor! I hate seeing myself talk on camera – I look ridiculous!"

    Click here for more celebrity children turned modelsThe Los Angeles-born beauty admitted that she had turned to her parents for advice on becoming a successful model, particularly looking to mother Jennifer Flavin who used to be a model herself. "She's like my wingman," said Sistine. "She's taught me my good side, my bad side, how to walk for the runway, and how to become someone else in a certain outfit."

    However, it seems that the pretty teen will also be looking to Sylvester for advice. "Modelling is basically acting, changing personalities," she said. "You don't want to be yourself." Sistine recently wowed on the red carpet when she joined her parents along with her sisters Sophia Rose and Scarlet Rose for the premiere of her father's film. v Sylvester joked with the press about the real reason his daughters were joining him at the glitzy event. "They're just here to see Kellan [co-star Kellan Lutz]. Can you blame them?" He also revealed that he has a unique way of judging whether a film has the potential to be a success. "I usually grade the quality of a film by the intensity of the injury," said the star. "When I do I break my neck, my spine, and dislocate shoulders. I fell on my back and had to put some metal in there, so if I'm squeaky, then deal with it, it's not my shoe, it's my back".
