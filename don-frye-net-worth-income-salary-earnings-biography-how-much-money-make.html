Don Frye Net Worth, Income, Salary, Earnings, Biography, How much money make?

Donald “Don” Frye (November 23, 1965) is a retired mixed martial artist, amateur and professional wrestler, and actor from the United States. He was one of the first well-rounded competitors in MMA, winning the UFC 8 and Ultimate Ultimate 96 competitions and coming second in the UFC 10 tournament in his first year of competition. Despite his achievements, he left MMA in 1997 to pursue a career in professional wrestling with New Japan Pro Wrestling, where he rapidly rose through the ranks to become one of the company’s top heels. Frye returned to MMA with the Pride Fighting Championships in September 2001, considerably more muscled and wearing an American patriot character in reaction to the September 11 attacks, after spending four years as one of Japan’s best gaijin wrestlers. During his two-year tenure in Pride, Frye earned a reputation for tenacity and fighting spirit, thanks to memorable fights with Ken Shamrock and Yoshihiro Takayama. He left the company in 2004 to participate in K-1 and Hero’s, but returned for the last Pride event in 2007.
