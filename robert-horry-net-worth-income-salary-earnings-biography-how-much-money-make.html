Robert Keith Horry, Jr. (/ri/; born August 25, 1970) is a former American basketball player who is now working as a sports pundit in the United States. He played in the National Basketball Association (NBA) for 16 seasons, earning seven championships, the most of any player who did not play for the Boston Celtics in the 1960s. He retired after his 16th season. Only two players (the other is John Salley) have won NBA championships with three different teams: twice with the Houston Rockets, three times with the Los Angeles Lakers, and twice with the San Antonio Spurs. 

He is one of only two players (the other is John Salley) to have done so with three different teams. As a result of his clutch shooting in crucial games, he acquired the moniker “Big Shot Rob.” He is generally regarded as one of the greatest clutch performers and victors in the history of the National Basketball Association (NBA). Horry is now employed as a sports analyst for Time Warner Cable SportsNet.
