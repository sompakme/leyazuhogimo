Whoopi Goldberg’s Early Life

Whoopi Goldberg was born on November 13, 1955, in Manhattan, New York City. She dropped out of high school and worked various jobs before she discovered her passion for acting. She honed her skills in acting, comedy, and singing by performing in theater productions and stand-up comedy.

Whoopi Goldberg’s Career

Goldberg’s career took off in the 1980s with her one-woman show, “Whoopi Goldberg.” Her performance caught the attention of director Steven Spielberg, who cast her in the lead role of his film “The Color Purple.” The film was a commercial success and earned Goldberg an Academy Award nomination.

Acting Career

Goldberg’s acting career includes a wide range of roles in both film and television. She has starred in over 150 films, including “Ghost,” for which she won an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress. Her other notable films include “Sister Act,” “The Lion King,” and “Girl, Interrupted.”

Television Career

In addition to her film career, Goldberg has also had a successful career in television. She is best known for her role as a co-host on the talk show “The View,” for which she has won a Daytime Emmy Award. She has also made appearances on popular television shows like “Star Trek: The Next Generation” and “Glee.”

Authorship and Other Ventures

Goldberg is also a successful author, with several books to her name. Her books, which include children’s books, non-fiction, and humor, have contributed to her net worth. Additionally, she has ventured into producing and has a production company, One Ho Productions.
