Evonne Goolagong Cawley, AO, MBE (born 31 July 1951) is an Australian former professional tennis player who reached the top of the world rankings. She was one of the world’s top players throughout the 1970s and early 1980s, when she won 14 Grand Slam titles: seven in singles (four Australian Open, two Wimbledon, and one French Open), six in women’s doubles, and one in mixed doubles. She retired from professional tennis after the 1980 season. Her career win-loss record was 704–165, good for an 81.01 percent win-loss percentage. Her overall win-loss record in Grand Slam singles tournaments was 82.09 percent (133–29); at the French Open, she had an 84.21 percent (16–3) record; at Wimbledon, she had an 83.33 percent (50–10) record; at the US Open, she had an 81.25 percent (26–6) record; and at the Australian Open, she had an 80.39 percent (41) record.
